Road Bike

A road bike is a type of bicycle that is designed for use on paved roads and is optimized for speed, efficiency, and agility. Road bikes typically feature a lightweight frame, narrow tires with high air pressure, drop handlebars that allow for multiple hand positions, and a gear system that enables the rider to pedal at high speeds with less effort. They are commonly used for recreational cycling, racing, and commuting, and are well-suited for long distance rides on paved surfaces. Road bikes are often distinguished from other types of bicycles by their focus on speed and efficiency, rather than comfort or off-road capabilities.
