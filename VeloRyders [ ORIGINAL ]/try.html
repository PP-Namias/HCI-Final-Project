Maintenance



TAKE NOTE: Maintaining a bike is important to ensure that it remains in good condition, performs well, and is safe to ride. Regular bike maintenance can help prevent issues like flat tires, chain wear, and brake problems, as well as increase the lifespan of the bike.




Here are examples of bike problems that require maintenance:

Broken Mud protector


Description and Cause:
The next type of bicycle damage is a broken mud shield. The mudguard has a function to protect the stanchion fork from splashing
mud while on the road.

Solution:
The material of this mudguard is made of ABS carbon fiber with a weight of 100 grams. If the mudguard breaks, then you can make repairs by replacing the broken shield.

Broken Chain Guard


Description and Cause:
Chain guards are enclosures for bicycle chains that are commonly found on utility bikes.


Solution:
If the chain guard breaks, it is better to buy a new one. Today there are many online shops that sell quality guard chains at very
affordable prices. The specifications for the generic part category is 75 grams.

Broken Chain


Description and Cause:
A broken chain is a serious problem that can cause a bike to suddenly stop and become unrideable. It occurs when one or more links in the chain break or separate, causing the chain to become disconnected and the bike to lose power.


Solution:
Replace new chain link if the chain is still functional if the chain is damaged, replace your chain.

Flat tire


Description and Cause:
A flat tire is a common problem that can occur when the air pressure in the tire drops or the tire becomes punctured, causing it to lose air. This can cause the tire to go flat and make the bike difficult or impossible to ride.


Solution:
Go to vulcanize if it can seal with patch kit or; if the interior has major damage, just change the interior.

Squeaky brakes


Description and Cause:
Squeaky bike brakes can be a frustrating and sometimes dangerous problem for cyclists. A squeaky sound coming from the brake system typically occurs when the brake pads and/or the rim or rotor surface are not making proper contact.


Solution:
To fix that, you muse clean your brakepods or replace your brakepods.

Loose spokes


Description and Cause:
The main reason why bike spokes get loose is that there is either too little tension in the wheel, or that the tension in the wheel is uneven.


Solution:
Tighten the spokes or replace it.

Loose pedals


Description and Cause:
Loose pedals on a bike can be a serious problem as they can cause a loss of control and lead to accidents. Loose pedals can occur due to various reasons. Accident or losing of threads


Solution:
To fix that, you must tight the crank arm, crank pedal and the whole cranksets.

Misaligned handlebars and stem


Description and Cause:
Misaligned handlebars and stem can be a serious issue that can cause discomfort, instability, and even accidents while riding a bike. The handlebars and stem are essential components that connect the front wheel and fork to the rest of the bike frame and allow the rider to steer and control the bike.


Solution:
To fix that, just tighten the bolt in stem, handlebar and if the bars has get lose thread, change it.

Slipping gears and chain skipping


Description and Cause:
Chain skipping and gear slipping are two common issues that can occur with a bike's drivetrain.


Solution:
To fix that, just align the gear to right position.

Loose/damaged bolts on bike frame


Description and Cause:
Loose or damaged bolts on a bike frame can be a serious safety issue that can cause instability, malfunction, and even accidents while riding a bike. The bolts on a bike frame are responsible for holding various components together, including the wheels, handlebars, brakes, and seat post.


Solution:
To fix that, just inspect the whole bike frame, check the loosen bolts and tight it.
