Valve

The inner tube of a bicycle tire is inflated or deflated through a valve, which extends through a hole in the wheel rim. Two commonly used valve types are Schrader and Presta. Schrader valves are found on bicycles, cars, and motorbikes, while Presta valves are exclusive to bicycles. These valves allow for the convenient addition or release of air from the inner tube, enabling riders to adjust tire pressure according to their preferences and riding conditions.
