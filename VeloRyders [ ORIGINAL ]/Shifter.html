Shifter

A shifter is a component on a bicycle that enables the rider to change gears. It is typically located on the handlebars and controls the movement of the front and/or rear derailleur.

Different frame materials have different properties that can affect weight, stiffness, and ride quality, and the geometry of the frame can have an impact on the bike's stability, agility, and responsiveness. Additionally, frames can vary in shape and design to optimize performance for each type of riding, including road cycling, mountain biking, and touring.The diamond frame, which consists of 6 tubes shaped into two connected triangles, is the most popular type of frame:

By using the shifter, the rider can shift the chain onto different chainrings or cassette cogs, adjusting the gear ratio. Different types of shifters exist, including traditional shifters for derailleurs, twist shifters, trigger shifters, and electronic shifters. The shifter plays a crucial role in allowing the rider to adapt to various terrains and cycling conditions by selecting the appropriate gear ratio.
