Rim

A bicycle rim is the circular outer component of the wheel that provides structural support and contains the tire. Nowadays, aluminum alloy is the most often utilized material, providing a good blend of strength, durability, and light weight.

Rim brakes have a flat surface on the sides of the rim that allows the brake pads to make contact with and hold the rim when applied. This friction slows or stops the bike by applying pressure to the rim. The smooth brake surface offers good braking performance as well as rim wear resistance over time.
