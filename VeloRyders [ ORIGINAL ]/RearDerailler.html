Rear Derailleur

The rear derailleur is a crucial component of a geared bike that facilitates the shifting of the chain between different cassette cogs on the rear wheel. It works alongside the jockey wheel to achieve smooth and accurate gear changes.

By moving horizontally, the rear derailleur guides the chain from one cog to another, enabling the rider to adjust the gear ratio and find the appropriate resistance for their desired speed or terrain. However, it's important to note that the rear derailleur is not present on single-speed or fixed-gear bikes, as they only have one rear cog.
