Breaks

Bike brakes are essential for a bicycle to stop the bike safely, providing control and ensuring rider safety. There are various types of bike brakes commonly used.

Rim brakes: Rim brakes, also known as caliper brakes, are the most common type of brakes found on bicycles. They consist of brake pads that grip onto the wheel rims when the brake lever is squeezed. Rim brakes are lightweight, affordable, and easy to maintain. They are commonly used on road bikes, hybrid bikes, and commuter bikes.

Disc brakes, on the other hand, use a rotor attached to the wheel hub and calipers to squeeze brake pads onto the rotor. They offer excellent stopping power, perform well in various conditions, and are commonly used on mountain, road, and hybrid bikes.

The type of bike, riding style, terrain, and personal preferences all influence the choice of bike brakes. Each type of brake has its own set of benefits and considerations, so it's critical to select one that meets your requirements while also providing the desired degree of performance and safety.
