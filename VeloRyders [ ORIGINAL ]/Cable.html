Cable

Bike cables are essential for controlling the brakes and gears on a bicycle. There are two main types:

brake cables and gear cables. Brake cables run from the brake levers to the brakes mounted on the wheels, while gear cables connect the shifters on the handlebars to the front and rear derailleurs.

hey are made of strong and durable materials like steel or stainless steel, and are protected by outer casings to prevent damage from dirt, moisture, and friction. Regular maintenance, including lubrication and occasional replacement, ensures smooth cable operation. The specific routing and attachment points may vary depending on the bike, but the fundamental purpose remains consistent
