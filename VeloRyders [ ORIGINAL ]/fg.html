Fixed gear

A fixed-gear bike, also known as a fixed-wheel or fixie, is a type of bicycle that has a drivetrain with no freewheel mechanism. This means that the pedals are directly attached to the rear wheel, and the bike cannot coast or freewheel. In a fixed-gear bike, the rider is always pedaling when the bike is in motion, and stopping requires the rider to resist the motion of the pedals. The lack of a freewheel makes the bike more efficient in terms of power transfer and allows for better control and a closer connection between the rider and the bike.
