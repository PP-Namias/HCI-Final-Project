Mountain Bike

A mountain bike is a type of bicycle that is designed for off-road cycling on rugged terrain, such as mountains, trails, and forests. Mountain bikes typically feature a sturdy frame, wide tires with knobby tread for traction, a suspension system to absorb shocks, and flat or riser handlebars for better control. They are often equipped with a wide range of gears to help the rider navigate steep climbs and descents. Mountain bikes are versatile and can be used for a variety of purposes, including recreational riding, trail riding, and even competitive racing. They are generally heavier and more durable than road bikes, and are designed to handle the rigors of off-road riding.
