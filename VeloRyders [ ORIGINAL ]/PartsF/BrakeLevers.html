Break Levers

Brake levers are essential components of a bike's braking system. When you want to slow down or stop, you squeeze the brake levers with your fingers or hands, which activates the brakes. In some cases, brake levers are integrated with gear shifters, forming a combined unit known as a "brake shifter" or "brifter". The brake cables connect the brake levers to the brakes themselves. When you squeeze the brake levers, the tension in the brake cables increases, causing the brakes to engage.

This action applies pressure to the braking surface, resulting in friction that slows down the rotation of the wheels and ultimately brings the bike to a stop. Brake levers, along with the associated brake cables and brakes, are vital for safe and effective braking, providing control and responsiveness to the rider.
