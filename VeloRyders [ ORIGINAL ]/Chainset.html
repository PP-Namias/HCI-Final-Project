Chainset/ Crankset

The chainset/ crankset is a crucial component of a bicycle that converts the rider's leg power into rotational motion, propelling the bike forward. It is composed of several parts, including the chainrings and the crank arms.

The chainrings are the gears that the chain wraps around, while the crank arms are attached to the chainrings and serve as the connection point for the rider's pedals.

When the rider pushes down on the pedals, the crank arms transfer the force to the chainrings, causing them to rotate. The crankset is securely fastened to the bike frame through a component called the bottom bracket, which acts as a bearing and allows the crankset to rotate smoothly while maintaining stability and structural integrity.
